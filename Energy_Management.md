# Energy Management

## 1. Manage Energy, not Time

### Question 1 - What are the activities you do that make you relax - Calm quadrant?

**Answer:**  
- Listening to soft music  
- Taking a slow walk  
- Deep breathing  
- Sitting quietly without screens  
- Meditation or doing nothing  
- Light stretching or yoga  
- Spending quiet time in nature  
- Reading a calming book  

### Question 2 - When do you find getting into the Stress quadrant?

**Answer:**  
- Learning something challenging  
- Facing deadlines or urgent tasks  
- Multitasking too much  
- Overloaded schedule  
- Physical tiredness or sleep deprivation  
- Rushing through work or studies  
- Worrying about outcomes instead of actions  

### Question 3 - How do you understand if you are in the Excitement quadrant?

**Answer:**  
- Feeling energized and motivated  
- Curious and eager to work  
- Losing track of time due to enjoyment  
- Mind feels sharp and focused  
- Feeling happy anticipation  
- Can work intensely but still feel good

## 2. Sleep is Your Superpower

### Question 4 - Paraphrase the Sleep is your Superpower in your own words in brief.

**Answer:**  
- Sleep boosts learning and memory  
- Sleep removes toxins from the brain  
- Lack of sleep weakens immunity  
- Insufficient sleep impacts heart health  
- Sleep improves emotional balance  
- Poor sleep increases cancer risk  
- Deep sleep enhances creativity and problem-solving  

### Question 5 - What are some ideas that you can implement to sleep better?

**Answer:**  
- Maintain a consistent sleep schedule  
- Avoid screens 1 hour before bed  
- Create a cool, dark, quiet sleep environment  
- Do light exercise during the day  
- Avoid heavy meals before bed  
- Practice relaxation (breathing, meditation)  
- Keep a wind-down routine  
- Get morning sunlight exposure

## 3. Brain-Changing Benefits of Exercise

### Question 6 - Brain Changing Benefits of Exercise.

**Answer:**  
- Exercise boosts neurotransmitters (serotonin, dopamine)  
- Increases BDNF, supporting brain growth  
- Enhances memory and learning by strengthening the hippocampus  
- Reduces stress and anxiety  
- Protects the brain from aging and cognitive decline  
- Improves focus and attention  

### Question 7 - What are some steps you can take to exercise more?

**Answer:**  
- Start with small movements (10–15 min walk)  
- Schedule exercise in your daily routine  
- Choose activities you enjoy (sports, dancing, yoga)  
- Use short workouts on busy days (5–10 min)  
- Keep workout clothes ready  
- Track progress for motivation  
- Find an exercise buddy  
- Take movement breaks to reduce sitting  
- Use stairs instead of elevators
