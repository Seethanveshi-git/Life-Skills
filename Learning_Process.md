## **Question 2 - What was the most interesting story or idea for you?**

### **Answer:**
The most interesting idea is how the brain has two modes, focused and diffuse, and how switching between them helps in deeper learning and better problem solving.

---

## **Question 3 - What are active and diffused modes of thinking?**

### **Answer:**
- **Active (Focused) Mode:**  
  A concentrated, deliberate thinking mode used when you study, code, or directly solve problems.

- **Diffused Mode:**  
  A relaxed, broad-thinking mode where your brain forms creative connections when you are not intensely focusing like walking or resting.

---

## **Question 4 - What are the steps to take when approaching a new topic?**

### **Answer:**
- Choose a specific topic.  
- Break it into smaller sub-skills.  
- Learn enough to self-correct.  
- Remove barriers to practice.  
- Practice intensely and consistently.

---

## **Question 5 - What are some of the actions you can take going forward to improve your learning process?**

### **Answer:**
- Use the Feynman Technique to understand concepts deeply.  
- Switch between focused and diffused thinking.  
- Break concepts into sub-skills and practice them.  
- Maintain deep work: learn with 100% involvement and attention.  
- Remove distractions (notifications, social media, unnecessary apps).  
- Track your time using productivity apps (e.g., Boosted).  
- Implement what you learn through code or real examples.  
- Explain concepts in your own words and try teaching them.  
- Set helpful deadlines to guide your learning time.  
- Approach learning with curiosity, clarity, and joy.
