# Listening and Active Communication – Questions & Answers

---

## 1. Active Listening

### **Question 1 - What are the steps/strategies to do Active Listening?**

### **Answer:**
- Pay full attention to the speaker without distractions.  
- Maintain eye contact to show engagement.  
- Use body language and small verbal responses (e.g., “hmm”, “okay”) to show you are listening.  
- Do not interrupt, let the person finish speaking.  
- Ask clarifying questions when needed. 
- Notice the speaker’s tone, emotions, and body language.  
- Keep an open mind and avoid judging while listening.

---

## 2. Reflective Listening

### **Question 2 - According to Fisher’s model, what are the key points of Reflective Listening?**

### **Answer:**
- Repeat or rephrase what the speaker said to confirm understanding.  
- Reflect the speaker’s emotions, not just their words.  
- Stay neutral and avoid judging or reacting emotionally.  
- Ask questions to remove confusion.  
- Confirm with the speaker that your understanding is correct.  
- Pay attention to voice tone, expressions, and body language.

---

## 3. Reflection

### **Question 3 - What are the obstacles in your listening process?**

### **Answer:**
- Getting distracted by phone or surroundings  
- Thinking about my reply instead of listening  
- Daydreaming or losing focus  
- Making assumptions before the person finishes  
- Interrupting due to impatience  
- Stress or emotional reactions reducing concentration  

---

### **Question 4 - What can you do to improve your listening?**

### **Answer:**
- Keep my phone aside during conversations  
- Practice staying mentally present  
- Take notes during important meetings  
- Ask clarifying questions instead of assuming  
- Avoid interrupting and give the speaker time  
- Paraphrase or summarize what I heard  
- Stay patient and control emotional reactions  

---

## 4. Types of Communication

### **Question 5 - When do you switch to Passive communication style in your day-to-day life?**

### **Answer:**
- When I want to avoid conflict  
- When I feel the other person might get angry  
- When I am unsure or lack confidence  
- When I don’t want to hurt someone’s feelings  
- When I am tired or want to end conversations quickly  

---

### **Question 6 - When do you switch into Aggressive communication style in your day-to-day life?**

### **Answer:**
- When I am stressed or frustrated  
- When I feel disrespected or ignored  
- When I lose patience  
- When I strongly believe I am right  
- When I react emotionally instead of thinking calmly  

---

### **Question 7 - When do you switch into a Passive-Aggressive communication style in your day-to-day life?**

### **Answer:**
- When I don’t want to confront someone directly  
- When I feel unfairly treated  
- When I say “I’m fine,” but I’m actually upset  
- When I gossip instead of addressing the issue  
- When I use silent treatment to express anger  

---

### **Question 8 - How can you make your communication assertive?**

### **Answer:**
- Use “I” statements to express your feelings clearly.  
- Be direct and honest without hurting others.  
- Maintain calm and confident body language.  
- Learn to say “No” respectfully and set boundaries.  
- Listen actively before you respond.  
- Avoid extreme behaviors (too passive or too aggressive).  
- Speak clearly and confidently.  
- Respect your needs as well as others’ needs.
